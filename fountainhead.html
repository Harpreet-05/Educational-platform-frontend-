About the Book

"The Fountainhead" is a novel by Ayn Rand, first published in 1943. It follows the life of Howard Roark, an individualistic young architect who chooses to struggle in obscurity rather than compromise his artistic and personal vision.

About the Author

Ayn Rand was a Russian-American writer and philosopher. She is best known for her philosophy of Objectivism and her novels that emphasize individualism and the importance of pursuing one's own happiness.

Main Themes

  • Individualism vs. Collectivism
  • Integrity in Art and Life
  • The Role of Ego in Creativity
  • Society’s Resistance to Change
